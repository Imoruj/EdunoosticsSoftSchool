## 1. Executive Summary

Edunostics is a school-focused platform designed to help Nigerian schools manage the full academic workflow from setup to score entry, score review, broadsheets, report card publishing, attendance, behaviour ratings, lessons, quizzes, assignments, and parent-ready results.

Many schools still rely on spreadsheets, manual formatting, printed templates, WhatsApp follow-ups, and isolated files to manage academic records. This creates pressure at the end of every term. Scores are scattered across teachers. Broadsheets require manual formatting. Report card comments are collected late. Attendance records are stored separately. Administrators struggle to know what is complete and what still needs attention.

Edunostics solves this by giving the school one structured system.

With Edunostics, school leaders can:

- Track academic health from a central dashboard.
- Manage students, teachers, classes, subjects, sessions, and terms.
- Configure grading rules, score components, and report card templates.
- Let teachers enter scores directly or through templates.
- Review and broadcast scores before publishing results.
- Generate branded report cards and broadsheets.
- Track attendance, behaviour, affective traits, and psychomotor skills.
- Create lessons, quizzes, assignments, and scheme of work.
- Control user access by role.
- Support parent and student access to academic outcomes.

Edunostics is more than a report card generator. It is a complete academic operations system.

## 2. The Problem Schools Face

### Result Processing Is Often Fragmented

In many schools, result processing happens across different files and people:

- Teachers record scores in notebooks or spreadsheets.
- Administrators merge class scores manually.
- Broadsheets are formatted separately.
- Report cards are generated late.
- Comments are copied from different sources.
- Attendance records are managed outside the result workflow.
- Parent communication happens after the system is already under pressure.

This makes the result cycle slow, stressful, and error-prone.

### School Leaders Lack Real-Time Visibility

Proprietors and administrators often ask:

- Which class has completed score entry?
- Which teacher has not submitted?
- Which subjects are missing coverage?
- Which reports are ready to publish?
- Which attendance records are missing?
- Which class or subject is performing poorly?

Without a dashboard, these questions are usually answered too late.

### Manual Report Formatting Takes Too Much Time

Report cards and broadsheets are official school documents. They must be accurate, branded, structured, and consistent. Manual formatting increases the risk of errors and delays.

Schools need a workflow where scores, attendance, traits, comments, photos, signatures, grading rules, and templates work together.

Edunostics supports a complete term result workflow.

- Current term.
- Term dates.
- Classes.
- Class arms.
- Subjects.
- Teachers.
- Students.

- Report card template.
- Broadsheet template.

### Step 2: Enter Scores

Teachers enter scores by:

- Academic session.
- Term.
- Class arm.
- Subject.
- Student.

The platform records score components such as CA 1, CA 2, exam, total, grade, and remark.

### Step 3: Review Scores

Administrators or assigned reviewers inspect subject submissions before results are used for report cards.

This helps reduce errors and keeps score approval separate from initial entry.

### Step 4: Broadcast Scores

Approved scores move into the report workflow.

This makes it clear which subjects are ready and which are still pending.

### Step 5: Generate Broadsheet

The school generates class-level broadsheets for review.

### Step 6: Preview Report Cards

Report cards can be previewed before printing or publishing.

### Step 7: Publish Results

The school publishes finalized reports and can generate selected reports for download.

Yes. Role access can be configured for school admin, proprietor, class teacher, subject teacher, parent, and student roles.
